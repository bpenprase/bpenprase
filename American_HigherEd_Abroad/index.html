About the Book

In a world where political and cultural divisions seem increasingly entrenched and global challenges demand collaborative solutions, universities are our best hope for engaging with a diversity of cultures, ideas, and identities. American Higher Education Abroad: Lessons in Culture Curriculum and Community describes how the American model of the university, as it is adapted to different global contexts, works to leverage the power of higher education in bridging divides.

As centrifugal forces separate countries and cultures, this monograph delves into the transformative role of universities explicitly focused on engaging with and across a spectrum of beliefs, nationalities, and identities. Through the lens of leading global campuses, the book unveils how these institutions are not just academic communities but catalysts for cultivating global leadership and intercultural dialogue.

Book Parts & Chapters

Introduction

The Idea of the Global American University and Fostering Cosmopolitanism and Global Citizenship in the 21st Century

by Bryan Penprase (SUA)

Part I: Theoretical Foundations & Visionary Ideals

▼

Part I of the book explores the theoretical underpinnings and visionary ideals behind six global campuses. It scrutinizes the essence of a global education that encourages students to appreciate their cultural heritage while placing each heritage in a global context. This section interrogates the literature of cosmopolitanism and global citizenship, offering frameworks for articulating the value of a global education to a diverse audience of students, faculty, staff, and the broader public.

Part II: Curricular & Co-Curricular Innovations

▼

Part II examines the curricular and co-curricular innovations designed to facilitate multicultural understanding within the classroom and beyond. This segment provides a scaffold for addressing multicultural issues, especially pertaining to international students' unique educational assumptions. The book includes materials for intercultural teaching, guides for structuring discussion and collaboration within a global classroom, and the most effective techniques for surfacing and leveraging the strength of a diverse and international classroom. Amid rising international student populations, the insights from global campuses enhance student learning, engagement, retention, and success.

Part III: Student Experiences, Community & Belonging

▼

Part III explores on-the-ground student experiences of residential colleges, the nuanced process of hiring local staff, and the complexities of diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives in a global context. A pivotal focus is on experiential and peer-to-peer learning as indispensable tools for fostering a sense of belonging and community. Through compelling case studies of orientations, town halls, peer mentorship, and the active involvement of student leaders, this part brings to life the vibrancy of campus life while addressing the critical need to amplify voices of the entire student body, especially those from marginalized backgrounds.

Conclusion

The Future of Global Universities and Lessons for American Higher Education

by Bryan Penprase, Kyle Farley and Sara Pervaiz Amjad

About the Editors

Dr. Bryan Edward Penprase

Vice President for Sponsored Research and External Academic Relations and Professor of Astronomy, Soka University of America

Bryan E. Penprase is a scholar of higher education, technology, and innovation with extensive experience in global education initiatives. He is the author of multiple books on higher education transformation and frequently speaks at international conferences on educational innovation, technology integration, and the future of learning.

Dr. Kyle Farley

Partner, Cross Borders Education

Kyle Farley brings extensive expertise in global higher education initiatives and cross-cultural educational leadership. With experience across multiple global campuses, he contributes deep insights into building effective international learning communities.

Sara Pervaiz Amjad

Associate Dean of Student Affairs, Director of Student Life and Spiritual Life, Intercultural Education, & Conflict Transformation (SLICCET), NYU Abu Dhabi

Sara Pervaiz Amjad leads efforts in fostering inclusive communities and intercultural dialogue at one of the world's leading global universities. Her work focuses on creating belonging and addressing the complexities of diversity, equity, and inclusion in global educational contexts.
