About the Book

Amid widespread uncertainty about higher education and fears about AI's impact on our future, Future-Proofing the University provides a hopeful, forward-looking vision. Prof. Bryan Penprase explores how institutions worldwide are proactively responding to unprecedented technological advances and shifting societal demands.

Building upon his earlier acclaimed work, The New Global Universities – Reinventing Higher Education in the 21st Century (Princeton), this book delves deeper into how universities—from liberal arts colleges to innovative global institutions—are transforming their missions, teaching methods, and business models. Richly illustrated with real-world examples, interviews, and vivid campus descriptions, it highlights how universities creatively integrate AI, online learning, and project-based education to reach new students, particularly across Asia and the Global South.

The book examines universities that are transforming the future - including some of the most prestigious universities that are expanding our conceptions of how education can engage with a rapidly changing world, and other universities such as ASU, WGU and SNHU dedicated to expanding access to millions of new students. It explores new conceptions of how the university can be developed to leverage new technologies such as AI, micro credentials and virtual reality to transform learning and dramatically improve the efficiency and lower the costs of higher education.

Through exclusive interviews and extensive research conducted at Harvard's Graduate School of Education and Stanford's Digital Education Lab, Penprase showcases practical innovations reshaping curricula, faculty teaching methods, and hybrid learning models. He also addresses the transformative potential of AI, emphasizing the critical importance of human-centric education that fosters empathy and creativity.

Ultimately, Future-Proofing the University paints an inspiring portrait of higher education's potential—where institutions evolve to meet future challenges, harmonizing technology with the uniquely human aspects of learning.

About the Author

Dr. Bryan Penprase is Vice President for External Academic Relations and Sponsored Research at Soka University of America. He previously served as Dean of Faculty at Soka, Professor of Science and founding Director of Yale-NUS College's Teaching and Learning Center, and as a professor at Pomona College for two decades. His extensive research and academic work, including fellowships at Yale and Caltech, underpin his insights into educational innovation.

Penprase holds degrees from Stanford (BS, MS) and the University of Chicago (PhD), and is the author of several influential books on higher education and science, and writes regular columns on the future of higher education for Forbes.com.
